## Description:Family relationships under this implementation are defined in terms of parent-child and gender facts plus rules governing the identification of grandparents, siblings, cousins, children, and descendants. Recursion and logical inference is used to find out both direct and indirect antecedent connections including landmarks.
 * 
/* ===== Basic Facts ===== */

parent(john, mary).

/* ===== Derived Relationships ===== */

% finding if a person is a grandparent of another
grandparent(X, Y) :-
    parent(X, Z),
    parent(Z, Y).

% finding if two people share the same parent but are different
sibling(X, Y) :-
    parent(Z, X),
    parent(Z, Y),
    X \= Y.

% finding if two people are cousins by checking if their parents are siblings
cousin(X, Y) :-
    parent(P1, X),
    parent(P2, Y),
    sibling(P1, P2),
    X \= Y.

% finding the children of a given person
child(X, Y) :-
    parent(X, Y).

% finding all descendants of a person including through multiple generations
descendant(X, Y) :-
    parent(X, Y).
descendant(X, Y) :-
    parent(X, Z),
    descendant(Z, Y).
